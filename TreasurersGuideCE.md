# Treasurer’s Guide  
**Conceptual Edition**  
**Hope Baptist Church of Nashville, Georgia**
***Revision: 1.0***
***Date: 29 April 2026***

1\.  Purpose.  This guide establishes the foundational accounting concepts and stewardship principles that govern all financial management at Hope Baptist Church. It defines what must be accomplished and why, rather than prescribing specific procedures or tools. Detailed operational procedures will be developed later.

2\.  Fund Accounting – The Cornerstone of Church Stewardship  
2.1.  Churches must practice fund accounting to properly honor donor intent and maintain transparency.  
2.2.  Resources are categorized based on the presence or absence of donor-imposed restrictions.

2.2.1.  Net Assets Without Donor Restrictions (WODR)  
General operating resources that church leadership may use for any legitimate ministry or operational purpose.

2.2.2.  Net Assets With Donor Restrictions (WDR)  
Resources that may be used only for the specific purpose designated by the donor. Most such funds are temporarily restricted and become available for general use only when the donor’s purpose has been fulfilled.

2.3.  GAAP Context (ASU 2016-14)  
External financial statements present net assets in two classes only. Internally, the church maintains detailed visibility into individual restricted funds to uphold donor intent.

2.4.  Core Stewardship Imperative  
Donor restrictions are binding. Any expenditure of restricted resources for a purpose other than the donor’s intent constitutes a serious breach of trust and stewardship.

3\.  Revenue and Contribution Recognition  
3.1.  Contributions represent the primary source of the church’s financial support.  
3.2.  Revenue is recognized when a gift is received or when an unconditional promise to give is made and collection is reasonably assured.

3.2.1.  Unrestricted contributions increase Net Assets Without Donor Restrictions.  
3.2.2.  Restricted or designated contributions increase Net Assets With Donor Restrictions.

3.3.  Pledges are recognized only when unconditional. Conditional promises are not recorded as revenue until the condition is substantially satisfied.

4\.  Expense Recognition and Functional Allocation  
4.1.  Expenses must be understood through two complementary lenses: natural classification and functional classification.

4.1.1.  Natural Classification identifies the type of resource consumed (salaries, utilities, supplies, etc.).  
4.1.2.  Functional Classification identifies the purpose of the expenditure:

4.1.2.1.  Program Services – direct ministry and outreach activities.  
4.1.2.2.  Management & General – administrative and operational support.  
4.1.2.3.  Fundraising – activities to generate contributions.

4.2.  Shared or indirect costs must be allocated using reasonable, consistent, and well-documented methods.

5\.  Key Categories of Church Funds  
5.1.  The church typically manages several distinct categories of resources, each carrying its own restriction profile:

5.1.1.  Tithes and Offerings (generally unrestricted)  
5.1.2.  Designated Gifts (temporarily restricted for specific ministries or projects)  
5.1.3.  Benevolence Funds (restricted for assistance to those in need)  
5.1.4.  Missions Funds (restricted for missionary support and outreach)  
5.1.5.  Capital and Building Funds (restricted for facilities and major acquisitions)

5.2.  Accurate identification and ongoing tracking of each fund’s restriction level is essential to faithful stewardship.

6\.  The Concept of Spending and Resource Allocation  
6.1.  Every expenditure must align with the church’s biblical mission, respect donor restrictions, and be appropriately classified.  
6.2.  Resources from restricted funds may be used only when the expense directly fulfills the donor’s stated purpose.  
6.3.  Overhead and shared costs require thoughtful allocation to ensure functional reporting reflects reality.  
6.4.  Strong documentation, appropriate approvals, and clear statements of purpose form the foundation of accountability and trust.
6.5.  Staff Compensation & Payroll
6.5.1.   Churches may provide compensation to pastors and staff through salaries, housing allowances, travel reimbursements, or other forms consistent with IRS guidelines and church bylaws.
6.5.2.   All staff compensation must be properly classified and recorded using double-entry accounting.
6.5.3.   Housing and travel allowances are typically recorded as expenses in the appropriate functional category (Program Services or Management & General).
6.5.4.   When the church begins processing W-2 payroll or 1099 payments, these transactions must follow proper segregation for audit and tax reporting purposes while maintaining donor intent.
6.5.5.   Detailed payroll procedures, tax compliance checklists, and related internal controls will be developed in separate operational documentation.

7\.  Budgeting as a Stewardship Discipline  
7.1.  The annual budget is a forward-looking expression of the church’s ministry priorities and a tool for responsible resource management.  
7.2.  It should reflect functional and natural expense categories while distinguishing between unrestricted operating resources and restricted funds.  
7.3.  Regular comparison of actual results to the budget enables leadership to exercise prudent oversight and maintain long-term financial health.

8\.  Period-End Financial Closing Concepts  
8.1.  Regular closing activities at month-end, quarter-end, and year-end ensure that financial records accurately reflect the church’s position and activities.  
8.2.  These processes include proper classification of transactions, timely recognition of releases from restrictions, and preparation of reliable reports.

9\.  Board Reporting and Audit Readiness  
9.1.  The Treasurer is responsible for providing clear, timely, and transparent financial information to church leadership and the board.  
9.2.  Reports should communicate net asset positions, functional expense allocation, budget performance, and the status of restricted funds.  
9.3.  High-quality supporting documentation and consistent application of these principles render the church’s records inherently audit-ready.

10\.  Release from Restrictions  
10.1.  When resources given under donor restriction are expended for their intended purpose, a reclassification must occur.  
10.2.  The amount is moved from Net Assets With Donor Restrictions to Net Assets Without Donor Restrictions.  
10.3.  This reclassification is the primary accounting expression of stewardship, visibly confirming that the church has honored the donor’s intent.

11\.  Donor Modification of Restrictions  
11.1.  A donor may modify or release a restriction only through clear written communication.  
11.2.  Such changes must be carefully documented and recorded as appropriate re-classifications, preserving a complete and transparent stewardship record.

12\.  Internal Controls and Overall Stewardship Mindset  
12.1.  Effective internal controls — including segregation of duties, proper approvals, and thorough documentation — safeguard the church’s resources and reinforce accountability.  
12.2.  The governing mindset for all financial activity is one of faithful stewardship: every decision and record must reflect care, integrity, transparency, and obedience to the trust placed in the church by its donors and by God.
